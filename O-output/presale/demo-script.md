# CX Mate — Beta Demo Script
**Format:** Zoom, founder-led, 15-20 min
**Audience:** Founders, COOs, Heads of CS at B2B companies (30-300 employees) — no CX expert on staff
**Product state:** Supervised demo only — not ready for unsupervised use
**Goal:** End with a clear next step (beta access commitment or follow-up call)

---

## Before You Open the Browser

Have this loaded and ready before the call:
- CX Mate tab open to the homepage (not your own company — use a demo company profile that's already generated, see demo-prep-checklist.md)
- Your own company's generated journey open in a second tab (if you want the "this is what it looks like for a company like mine" moment)
- Camera on. Screen share ready but NOT yet shared — wait for the right moment

---

## Opening (0:00 — 2:00)

### Do NOT open with a product tour. Open with a question.

After greetings, say this (adapt to what you know about them):

> "Before I show you anything — tell me, how do you currently think about your customer journey? Like, if I asked you today: where are the top three moments where you lose customers — do you know the answer?"

**Wait for their response. Really listen.**

This question does three things:
1. It signals this is not a feature demo — it's a conversation
2. Their answer tells you which persona they are and where to focus
3. It creates a gap — they feel the absence of the answer they can't give

**Then, based on their answer:**

If they say "we have some idea" → "And do you have a playbook for those moments? Something your team can act on consistently?"

If they say "not really" → "That's actually the most common answer I hear from companies at your stage. And the thing is, your customers have a journey whether you designed it or not. CX Mate maps what that actually looks like — and tells you what to do about it."

If they say "we have NPS / we do customer calls" → "Great. But NPS tells you the score — it doesn't tell you which moment caused it. That's the gap we close."

---

## The 3 Questions to Ask Before Showing Anything (2:00 — 4:00)

These anchor the demo to their reality. Ask them in order, naturally — not like a survey.

**Question 1: "Where are you in the journey right now?"**
(Pre-launch? First customers? Growing? How many customers approx?)

This tells you which CX maturity stage they map to and how to frame everything downstream.

**Question 2: "What's the thing keeping you up at night on the customer side?"**

Listen for: churn, onboarding friction, handoff breaks, not knowing why customers leave, team scaling faster than process. Note their exact words — use them back in the demo.

**Question 3: "Have you tried anything like this before — or is this a fresh space for you?"**

This reveals their competitive context and sets up the "why CX Mate is different" moment without it feeling defensive.

## The Setup (4:00 — 5:30)

Before sharing your screen:

> "What I'm going to show you is the full output — the journey map, the CX report, and the playbook. It takes about 5-8 minutes of onboarding to generate. I've already run it for [demo company name] so we can go through the outputs together.

> The thing that's different here: this isn't a template. It's built specifically for [demo company] — their stage, their pains, their competitors. What you'll see is what you'd actually get for [their company name]. Same process."

Share your screen now. Navigate to the demo company's dashboard or journey.

## The Live Walkthrough (5:30 — 14:00)

### Screen 1: The Dashboard (5:30 — 7:00)

Open to the Dashboard.

> "This is the dashboard — your CX home base. The big number at the top is the aggregate revenue impact range CX Mate found for [demo company]. Not made up — calculated from their specific data and industry benchmarks, with the math shown."

Point to the 4-stat strip (stages mapped, moments identified, critical risks, playbook items):

> "50+ moments across their full customer lifecycle. [X] of them flagged as critical or high-risk. This is where you'd normally need a CX consultant to spend weeks doing this analysis. It took 5 minutes of onboarding."

**Pause here.** Let the number land. Don't rush to the next screen.

If they lean in or say "interesting" — that's your cue to go deeper. If they look skeptical, say: "I know — the number looks big. That's why we show every formula and data source. You can see exactly how it's calculated."

---

### Screen 2: The Journey Map (7:00 — 9:30)

Navigate to /journey.

> "This is the journey map. Every stage from [first touch to advocacy / onboarding to renewal — adapt based on demo profile]. Each card is a meaningful moment — not a touchpoint, not a step in a process. A moment where your customer decides whether to stay or leave."

Click into one critical moment card.

> "Look at this one — [name the specific moment, e.g., 'The day-7 value gap']. This is the moment where [demo company]'s customers are most likely to disengage. The evidence badge here — [point to violet badge] — connects this back to a pain point they told us about in onboarding. It's not a generic recommendation. It's built from their reality."

**Ask them:** "If this were your company — does any of this ring true? Is [the named moment] something you think about?"

If yes: "That's exactly the point. You already know this intuitively. CX Mate gives it a name, quantifies it, and tells you what to do."

If no: "That's useful too — it means onboarding is stronger for you, and the risk is elsewhere. The system would surface that for your specific profile."

---

### Screen 3: The CX Intelligence Report (9:30 — 11:30)

Navigate to /confrontation.

> "This is the CX Intelligence Report — what we call the Confrontation page, on purpose. Because it shows you what's actually at risk, not what you want to hear."

Point to the Evidence Wall section:

> "Every finding here links back to what [demo company] told us — their pain points, their competitors, their stage. If they said 'our onboarding is weak,' that shows up as evidence woven through every relevant recommendation."

Point to the impact breakdown:

> "The impact projections: each one shows the calculation, the data source, and whether it's from their own data or an industry benchmark. This is not AI making up numbers. This is methodology made transparent."

**The confrontation moment** — say this directly:

> "Most CX tools show you dashboards of what happened. This shows you what's at risk and what to do before it happens. That's a different product."

---

### Screen 4: The Playbook (11:30 — 13:00)

Navigate to /playbook.

> "And this is the playbook — what Monday looks like. Every recommendation is prioritized by stage, with effort estimates, AI tool suggestions for each action, and evidence annotations connecting back to their specific context."

Point to a high-priority recommendation:

> "This one — [read the recommendation title]. Effort: [X]. Time to see results: [X]. And here's the AI tool that's best suited to implement it. This isn't 'improve your onboarding.' This is: here's the specific moment, here's the fix, here's how long it takes, here's what to use."

**The WOW moment — pause here.**

> "Take a second. This whole thing — journey map, CX report, playbook — was generated in under 10 minutes. A CX consultant would charge $15-30K to produce an analysis half this depth. And it would take 6-8 weeks."

Let the silence sit for 2-3 seconds. Do not fill it.

## Objection Handling — Embedded in the Right Moments

These come up during the walkthrough, not after. Handle them inline.

**"Is this really specific to us, or is it generic?"**
Best moment to address: When showing the journey map's evidence badges.
> "Look at the violet badges — each one connects back to something [demo company] told us. 'Addresses: churn risk.' 'vs [their competitor].' This is built from their inputs, not from a template."

**"How accurate are the revenue numbers?"**
Best moment to address: When showing the CX Intelligence Report impact section.
> "Every number shows its methodology — 'based on industry benchmark for SaaS Growing stage' vs 'your data.' We're not hiding the math. You can see exactly where the estimate comes from and challenge any assumption you want."

**"This looks powerful but we're too early / small / pre-revenue"**
Best moment to address: When you've learned their stage in the opening questions. Redirect before they object.
> "Actually — the best time to build this is before you have bad habits to undo. Pre-launch, first customers — that's when the journey you design becomes the one that compounds. The founders who wait until they have 100 customers regret the first 50."

**"We already have HubSpot / Salesforce / [CRM]"**
> "CRMs track transactions. CX Mate maps experiences. You'll keep your CRM — this sits on top of it and tells you what the data means for your customer relationships. Different problem."

## The Closing Sequence (14:00 — 17:00)

Do NOT ask "so what do you think?" — it's weak and puts the burden on them.

Instead, anchor back to their opening answer:

> "At the start, I asked you where you lose customers and you said [paraphrase their answer]. Did what you just saw map to that reality?"

**Then the direct ask:**

> "We're in closed beta right now — I'm personally selecting the first cohort. What I'm looking for are founders who are willing to run their real company through it, give us feedback on what's useful and what's not, and help shape the product. Would you be a fit for that?"

**If they say yes:**
> "Great. I'll send you the link to onboard after this call. Your company goes through the full flow — you'll have your journey map, report, and playbook in under 10 minutes. I'll follow up in 48 hours to hear what you found."

**If they hesitate:**
> "Totally fair. The product is still in beta — I want to be upfront about that. What would make this feel worth your 10 minutes?"

**If they want to buy (great problem):**
> "The Starter tier is $79/month — that gives you everything you just saw, plus the ability to save your results, track progress monthly, and get a Revenue Protected counter as you complete playbook items. I'll send you the link."

**Always end with a specific next step, a specific time, and your name on it:**
> "I'll send the link and a summary email within 2 hours. Sound good?"

## What NOT to Do in This Demo

- Do NOT apologize for the product being in beta. It's early — that's why they get access.
- Do NOT show the onboarding wizard in real-time. It takes 5-8 minutes of form-filling — demo time is precious. Show outputs, not inputs.
- Do NOT fill the silence after the wow moment. Let them feel it.
- Do NOT get into pricing details unless they ask. Lead with value, follow with price.
- Do NOT go longer than 20 minutes. Respect their time — it signals product confidence.
- Do NOT use the words "platform," "solution," "leverage," or "seamless."
