# Friendly Testers — Invite Flow & Communication

Your playbook for sharing CX Mate with your first testers. These are people you know — founders, CS leaders, operators in your network. The goal: real usage, honest feedback, and early signal on what works.

---

## Who to Invite (10-15 people max)

Pick testers who match at least one:

| Type | Why they're valuable | What you'll learn |
|------|---------------------|-------------------|
| **B2B founder with customers** | They have real pain. Their results will be meaningful. | Does the output match reality? |
| **Head of CS / CS Manager** | They'll judge the playbook quality — that's their job. | Are recommendations actionable? |
| **Founder pre-launch** | They'll test the "design your CX before launch" angle. | Does the pre-customer flow work? |
| **Someone who knows nothing about CX** | They'll expose confusing UX and jargon. | Is the onboarding clear enough? |
| **Someone technical** | They'll find bugs you missed. | What breaks? |

**Avoid:** People who'll say "looks great!" to be nice. You need honest signal.

---

## Step 1: The Invite (WhatsApp / DM / Text)

Keep it personal. No marketing voice. This is you asking a friend.

### Version A — For founders/operators you know well

> Hey [name]! I've been building something I'm really excited about — it's an AI tool that maps your entire customer journey and gives you a step-by-step playbook to improve it. Think of it like having a CX consultant on your team, but it takes minutes instead of months.
>
> I'm looking for 10-15 people to test it with their real company and tell me what's useful, what's confusing, and what's missing.
>
> Would you be up for trying it? Takes about 10 minutes. I'd love your honest take.
>
> ---
>
> **How to connect:**
> 1. Go to: https://cx-mate.vercel.app
> 2. Click **Log in** (top right)
> 3. Enter your email + password and sign in (no account yet? Click **Sign up** — takes 10 seconds)
>
> **What to do inside:**
> 1. Click **Start Onboarding** (or **Get Your Action Playbook**)
> 2. Enter your company name and website — the system auto-enriches your data
> 3. Answer the questions (about your company, customers, challenges) — the more specific, the better the output
> 4. Wait 1-2 minutes while the AI generates everything
> 5. Explore your results: **Journey Map** → **CX Report** → **Playbook**
>
> That's it! Once you're done, send me your thoughts.

## Step 3: The Feedback Ask (2-3 days after they try it)

Don't send a form. Have a conversation.

### Quick check-in (WhatsApp/DM)

> Hey [name]! Did you get a chance to try CX Mate? I'd love to hear what stood out — good or bad.
>
> If you have 2 minutes, the 3 things I'm most curious about:
>
> 1. **First reaction** — When you saw your results, what was your gut feeling? ("This is me" / "This is generic" / "I'm confused")
> 2. **The playbook** — Was there one recommendation you'd actually act on this week?
> 3. **What felt off** — Anything that confused you, felt wrong, or was missing?
>
> Even one sentence is super helpful. No pressure on a long reply.

### בעברית — בקשת פידבק

> היי [שם]! הספקת לנסות את CX Mate? אשמח לשמוע מה בלט — לטוב או לרע.
>
> אם יש לך 2 דקות, שלושה דברים שאני הכי סקרנית לגביהם:
>
> 1. **תגובה ראשונה** — כשראית את התוצאות, מה הייתה התחושה? ("זה אני" / "זה גנרי" / "לא הבנתי")
> 2. **ה-Playbook** — יש המלצה אחת שהיית באמת מיישם/ת השבוע?
> 3. **מה הרגיש לא בסדר** — משהו שבלבל, הרגיש לא נכון, או חסר?
>
> אפילו משפט אחד עוזר מאוד. בלי לחץ על תשובה ארוכה.

### If they didn't try it yet

> Totally get it — whenever you have 10 minutes, it's there for you. No rush.

### בעברית — אם עדיין לא ניסו

> לגמרי מבינה — מתי שיש לך 10 דקות, זה שם מחכה. בלי לחץ.

(Don't chase more than once. If they don't try it, they're not the right tester.)

## Step 4: The Deep Feedback (For testers who engaged)

If someone gives substantive feedback, ask for a 15-min call or voice note:

> This is really helpful — would you be open to a quick 15-min chat? I'd love to dig deeper into [specific thing they mentioned]. I can work around your schedule.

**On the call, explore:**
- What did they expect vs what they got?
- Did anything surprise them about their journey map?
- Would they share the playbook with their team? Why/why not?
- What would make them come back and use it again?
- Would they pay for this? At what price point would it feel like a no-brainer?

---

After you get feedback, close the loop. This builds the relationship for later.

> [name], thank you so much for the feedback — it's already shaping what we're building next.
>
> Quick update on what I'm doing with your input:

>

>
> Really appreciate you taking the time.

## What You're Listening For

| Signal | What it means | Action |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| "This is exactly what's happening in my company" | Journey mapping works. The output feels personalized. | Double down on specificity. |
| "The playbook is too generic" | Recommendations need more context-awareness. | Improve prompt with more onboarding data. |
| "I didn't understand what to do with the CX Report" | Information hierarchy is off. Users need a clearer "so what." | Redesign the report flow. |
| "I'd share this with my team" | The output has standalone value. | Add easy sharing/export. |
| "I wouldn't pay for this" | The free output is already enough, or the value isn't clear. | Sharpen the paid tier differentiation. |
| "I'd pay for this today" | Product-market fit signal. Dig deeper: what specifically? | Ask what price, what trigger. |
| Someone comes back without being asked | Retention signal. The strongest possible validation. | Find out what brought them back. |
