# CX Mate Beta — ICP Target List
*Created: 2026-03-08 | Owner: Growth*

---

## The Beta ICP in One Sentence

A B2B SaaS company at Series A that just hired or is about to hire their first CSM — and has no formal CX framework in place.

---

## Primary ICP Profile

| Attribute | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| **Business model** | B2B SaaS, subscription |
| **Stage** | Series A (raised $2M–$15M in last 18 months) |
| **Team size** | 15–60 employees |
| **Customer count** | 30–300 active customers |
| **CS maturity** | Founder-led CS or 1–3 CSMs, no formal journey, no dedicated CX hire |
| **Pain signals** | Churn they can't explain, onboarding inconsistency, handoff friction, first renewal anxiety |
| **Budget signal** | CS tools spend: $0–$500/mo (Notion, Intercom, a CRM) |
| **Buyer** | Founder / CEO, Head of CS, COO, CRO |
| **Why they're ready now** | Just got funding. Suddenly have 50+ customers. The "founder-led magic" isn't scaling. |

---

## Pain Signal Checklist (qualify fast)

A company is a strong beta candidate if they match 3+ of these:

- [ ] Listed a Customer Success Manager role in last 6 months
- [ ] Their LinkedIn shows "Head of CS" or "VP Customer Success" was hired in the last year
- [ ] They've written/posted about customer onboarding or churn on LinkedIn/blog
- [ ] 30–300 customers (public pricing page or Crunchbase customers field)
- [ ] No evidence of a Gainsight/Totango/ChurnZero contract
- [ ] Raised Series A in the last 18 months (Crunchbase/TechCrunch)
- [ ] B2B SaaS with a clear onboarding moment (not PLG-only)

## Target Verticals (in priority order)

### 1. HR Tech / Workforce Management SaaS
**Why:** HR SaaS buyers are demanding. Implementation is complex. Onboarding is make-or-break. Lots of Series A activity.
**Examples of company types:** Performance management tools, employee engagement platforms, workforce planning SaaS, scheduling/shift management

### 2. Revenue Operations / Sales Enablement
**Why:** Buyers understand CX (they build it). CSMs in this space are sophisticated. High willingness to adopt AI tools. Strong LinkedIn presence.
**Examples:** Conversation intelligence, proposal automation, CRM enrichment, sales coaching SaaS

### 3. Legal Tech / Compliance SaaS
**Why:** Complex onboarding. High-stakes renewals. Low CX sophistication — early mover advantage. Strong ROI story ("one churn costs us $80K ARR").
**Examples:** Contract management, compliance automation, entity management, legal workflow

### 4. FinTech for SMBs
**Why:** High churn risk. Competitive market. CS teams are small but hungry for tools. Strong use case for CX Review deliverable.
**Examples:** Expense management, AP automation, payroll SaaS, banking-as-a-service wrappers

### 5. Developer Tools / API Products
**Why:** "CX" is undervalued in devtools — which means they have the most room to improve. Often PLG companies discovering that enterprise sales need a different journey.
**Examples:** Monitoring tools, CI/CD, testing infrastructure, API management

### 6. Marketing Tech
**Why:** High churn industry. CS teams that understand data. Strong appetite for structured frameworks.
**Examples:** Email automation, SEO tools, content analytics, influencer platforms

## How to Find Them (10 minutes of research)

1. **Crunchbase:** Filter `Series A` + `B2B SaaS` + `raised in 2023-2025` → sort by employee count 15-60
2. **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** `Job title: Head of Customer Success OR VP Customer Success` + `Company size: 11-50` + `Industry: Software`
3. **LinkedIn feed signals:** Search "customer success" + "Series A" — people post when they hire CSMs
4. **AngelList / Wellfound:** Browse funded companies + CS job postings
5. **Twitter/X:** Search "first csm hire" or "scaling customer success" — founders announce this
