# CX Mate — Low-Radar Beta Campaign
*Created: 2026-03-08 | Owner: Growth / Anat*

---

## What "Low Radar" Means

No press releases. No ProductHunt. No paid ads. No spray-and-pray.

This is a peer-to-peer, problem-led, founder-voice campaign. You're not marketing a product — you're finding 15 people who have the exact problem you solve and handing them the solution. Every message feels like it comes from a founder who's been there, not a marketing team.

**3 channels. 3 weeks. 15–20 beta testers.**

---

## Channel 1: LinkedIn (Primary)

### Strategy
Anat posts as the founder — not CX Mate posts. *Her* feed, *her* voice, *her* observations about the CX problem. The product is the supporting evidence, not the lead.

2 posts per week for 3 weeks. Each post hits one pain point. The call to action is always "DM me" — not a link in the post (LinkedIn suppresses link posts).

**Hook:** The Gemini objection

```

The methodology is the moat. The AI is just what makes it fast enough to be worth trying.
```

## Channel 3: Direct Outreach (Precision Targeting)

*This is the sniper approach. Use the ICP list from `beta-icp-target-list.md`.*

For each qualified target:
1. Find them via LinkedIn or Crunchbase
2. Check for pain signals (CS hire, churn post, Series A announcement)
3. Personalize the first message to their specific situation
4. Use the DM templates from `beta-tester-outreach-sequence.md`

**Outreach cadence:** 5 new contacts per day, 3 days per week. That's 45 targeted touches over 3 weeks.

**Expected conversion:** 15–20% respond, 30–50% of responders try it → 3–6 active beta testers from direct alone.

## What NOT to Do

- ❌ Don't lead with the product name in cold DMs (nobody knows it yet)
- ❌ Don't put links in LinkedIn post body (suppressed by algorithm)
- ❌ Don't post in communities without reading the rules first
- ❌ Don't send the same message twice to the same person
- ❌ Don't use "AI-powered" as the hook — lead with the problem
- ❌ Don't post on ProductHunt yet — save that for the full public launch
- ❌ Don't run paid ads — this phase is about learning, not scaling
