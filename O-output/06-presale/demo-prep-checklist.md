# CX Mate — Demo Prep Checklist
**Purpose:** Run this before every demo, every time. Not optional.
**Owner:** Whoever is running the demo (currently: founder)
**Rule:** If you haven't done the day-before checks, postpone rather than demo cold.

---

## The Day Before

### Know Your Prospect (30 min, do this yourself — don't skim)

- [ ] Look up their LinkedIn profile. Note: current role, tenure, company stage, what they've posted about recently
- [ ] Look up the company website. Note: what they sell, who they sell to, how they describe their customer
- [ ] Check LinkedIn for company headcount — is it growing? Any CS / CX job posts?
- [ ] Look for any recent news: funding, product launches, executive hires
- [ ] Check if they have any CX / customer success / NPS content on their site — signals sophistication level
- [ ] Note one specific thing that makes their CX challenge unique — you'll use this in the opening

**Output:** One paragraph of notes. Paste it somewhere you'll see during the call.

---

### Prepare the Demo Profile (15 min)

The demo runs on a pre-generated company profile — NOT live onboarding during the call.

- [ ] Confirm the demo company profile is loaded and all outputs are generated:
  - Journey map: generates fully, no empty stages
  - CX Intelligence Report: impact projections visible, Evidence Wall populated
  - Playbook: at least 8-12 recommendations visible, evidence annotations showing
  - Dashboard: hero impact number visible, 4-stat strip populated
- [ ] Check that the demo profile matches the prospect's approximate profile:
  - If they're pre-launch: use a pre-launch demo profile
  - If they're Growing/Series A: use a growing stage demo profile
  - B2B SaaS profile preferred for most demos — switch to services/agency profile if relevant
- [ ] Know which specific moments and recommendations you'll highlight — pick 2-3 in advance, don't wing it
- [ ] If you plan to show your OWN company's journey as the "real" example: confirm it's generated and up to date

---

### Prepare Your Talking Points (10 min)

Review these files — don't re-read them in full, just the relevant sections:

- [ ] **Battlecard** (`O-output/presale/battlecard.md`): Review the section for any competitor they may have mentioned in your pre-call exchange. If none mentioned, re-read the HubSpot/Salesforce and "we'll build it internally" objections — they come up in almost every demo.
- [ ] **Demo script** (`O-output/presale/demo-script.md`): Re-read the 3 questions and the closing sequence. Know the wow moment timing.
- [ ] **One-pager** (`O-output/presale/one-pager.md`): Drop it in Zoom chat within the first 2 minutes of the call. Have it open in a browser tab.

---

### Technical Setup (5 min)

- [ ] Confirm your internet connection is stable. Switch to ethernet if possible.
- [ ] Clear your browser — close any tabs you don't need. No embarrassing tab names visible in the browser strip.
- [ ] Set Zoom to hide non-video participants if it's just the two of you — cleaner experience
- [ ] Test screen share: can you share just the browser window, not your full desktop?
- [ ] Have the product URL bookmarked and ready to navigate without typing
- [ ] Have the one-pager PDF or link ready to drop in chat
- [ ] Silence notifications on your machine. All of them.

---

## 30 Minutes Before

### Mental Prep (5 min)

- [ ] Re-read your prospect notes (the paragraph you wrote yesterday)
- [ ] Know the one thing you want them to walk away with — pick one, not three
- [ ] Know the specific question you'll open with — based on what you know about their stage

### Product Checks (10 min)

- [ ] Load the demo company dashboard — confirm the page loads cleanly, no errors
- [ ] Click through: Dashboard → Journey Map → CX Intelligence Report → Playbook. All screens load?
- [ ] Check one journey stage card — does it open with moment detail? Evidence badges visible?
- [ ] Check one playbook recommendation — effort estimate, AI tool suggestion, evidence annotation all showing?
- [ ] Check the Evidence Wall — populated with at least 3-4 evidence items?
- [ ] If showing a second tab (your own company): load it now, confirm it's current

### Do NOT Show in This Demo

- [ ] **Do NOT show the onboarding wizard live** — it takes 8 minutes of form-filling. Show outputs, not inputs. If they ask to see how it works, describe it verbally and offer to have them run it themselves after the call.
- [ ] **Do NOT show loading states** — if generation were to run live, the ~5 min wait would kill momentum. Always use pre-generated data.
- [ ] **Do NOT show the mobile view** — it's not optimized for demo yet
- [ ] **Do NOT show any error states, empty states, or beta-flagged features** — navigate away immediately if something breaks
- [ ] **Do NOT demo while logged in as a real customer account** — always use the designated demo account

### Zoom Setup (5 min)

- [ ] Camera on, good lighting, background clean or blurred
- [ ] Audio test — no echo, no background noise
- [ ] Have the Zoom link ready to paste if they can't find the invite
- [ ] Have the follow-up email template open and ready to personalize immediately after the call ends

---

## During the Call — Quick Reference

**Opening:** Ask the question, don't open with a product tour
**Before sharing screen:** Set up what they're about to see (the 3 questions first)
**During walkthrough:** Pause after the wow moment — don't fill the silence
**Objections:** Handle inline, not at the end
**Closing:** Specific next step + specific time + your name on it

**If the product breaks:**
Don't panic. Say: "Let me pull up the other view" and navigate to a different section. If it's unrecoverable, say: "Let me send you the link after this call — you'll see it in context better when you can click through yourself." Turn the bug into an invitation.

---

## After the Call — Within 2 Hours

- [ ] Send the follow-up email (see `O-output/presale/follow-up-email.md`) — personalized, not the template
- [ ] Log the call notes somewhere (even a Notion page or Notes app): what they said, what resonated, what objection came up, what they need before deciding
- [ ] Note what to watch out for in your own product: did anything break? Did a feature feel weak on demo? Flag it.

---

## Red Flags That Mean Reschedule

If any of these are true 30 min before the call — reschedule rather than demo under these conditions:

- The demo profile outputs are broken or generating incorrectly
- You haven't had time to research the prospect at all
- Your internet is flaky and you can't switch to ethernet
- You're flustered or distracted — a bad demo does more damage than a rescheduled one

**How to reschedule:** "I want to make sure this is the best 15 minutes of your week. I've hit a scheduling conflict — can we shift to [specific time]?" Keep it short, don't over-explain.
