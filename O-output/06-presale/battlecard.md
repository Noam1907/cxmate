# CX Mate — Competitive Battlecard
**Use:** When a prospect names a competitor or raises a "why not just..." objection
**Rule:** Never trash competitors. Reframe the category. Win on fit, not features.

---

## The Master Frame

Before any competitor comes up, plant this frame early:

> "There are two types of CX tools: ones that manage your existing customers reactively, and one that designs the journey proactively — before the problems happen. We're in the second category. Most tools are in the first."

This means when they mention Gainsight or ChurnZero, they've already heard the frame. You're not on defense — you're clarifying categories.

---

## Head-to-Head: Gainsight

**When they say:** "We've looked at Gainsight" / "Our investors mentioned Gainsight" / "Isn't this like Gainsight?"

**What to ask first:**
> "Are you planning to implement Gainsight, or just evaluating it?"

If evaluating:
> "What stage are you at? Because Gainsight is genuinely the best enterprise CS platform in the world — for companies with 200+ customers, a dedicated CS team, and 3-6 months to implement. The real implementation time is 12-24 weeks. The admin requirement is typically one full-time Gainsight admin per 5-10 CSMs. That's around $100K in headcount before you get value.

> CX Mate gives you the strategic intelligence Gainsight promises — without the implementation, without the admin, without the enterprise price tag. We're the foundation you build before you need Gainsight."

**What to say:**
- "Gainsight is the right answer for companies that need enterprise-scale CS management. We're the right answer for companies that aren't there yet — and don't want to wait 24 weeks to get started."
- "Think of us as what you build before you need Gainsight."

**What to never say:**
- "Gainsight is too expensive" (sounds defensive)
- "We're better than Gainsight" (we lose on features head-to-head, and they know it)
- "Gainsight is bad" (it has 2,500+ reviews for a reason)

**Our edges:**
- Time to value: one conversation vs. 12-24 weeks
- No admin hire required
- No CRM integration required to get started
- Built for founders, not CS teams

## Head-to-Head: ChurnZero

**When they say:** "We use ChurnZero" / "We're looking at ChurnZero"

**What to ask first:**
> "Do you have a CS team running it, or are you still in setup?"

If they have a working CS team using ChurnZero:
> "ChurnZero is solid for frontline CSMs managing accounts day-to-day. It's designed for that workflow. CX Mate does something different — it maps the journey BEFORE the accounts land on a CSM's desk. Less firefighting for your team if the moments are designed right upfront."

If they're evaluating ChurnZero:
> "ChurnZero's biggest G2 complaint — across 1,558 reviews — is reporting. Getting board-ready data out of it typically requires exporting to a BI tool. CX Mate builds the board-facing output in, from day one."

**What to say:**
- "ChurnZero tells you what happened to your customers. CX Mate tells you what will happen — and what to do before it does."
- "If you want to empower CSMs on existing accounts, ChurnZero makes sense. If you want to design the journey they're managing, that's us."

**What to never say:**
- "ChurnZero's reporting is broken" (too aggressive, they may have already bought it)
- "You don't need ChurnZero" (they might need both, and that's fine)

**Our edges:**
- No CRM data required to start
- Board-ready impact projections built in, not bolted on
- Journey design lens (proactive) vs. account management lens (reactive)
- Works before you have a CS team

## Head-to-Head: HubSpot / Salesforce

**When they say:** "Why can't we just use HubSpot for this?" / "We already have Salesforce"

**What to say:**
> "CRMs track transactions — deals, contacts, activities. CX Mate maps experiences — the emotional journey your customer has from first impression to loyal advocate. These are genuinely different problems.

> Your CRM will tell you a customer churned on March 15th. CX Mate tells you which moment six weeks earlier made them decide to leave. Different information. Different lever to pull."

**Then add:**
> "You'll keep HubSpot. In fact, our Pro tier pulls your HubSpot data in to validate our projections against your reality. They're complementary, not competing."

## The Big Objections (Not Competitor-Specific)

### "We'll just build this internally / have someone on the team do it"

**Don't dismiss this — validate, then reframe:**
> "You could. And some teams do build journey maps internally — it usually takes a consultant 6-8 weeks and $15-30K to do it at this depth. The question is: what's the best use of your team's time right now?

> CX Mate gives you a CCXP-level analysis in 10 minutes. Even if you wanted to build the methodology internally, you'd be using this as your starting framework — because it's built on 8 modules of structured CX theory, not someone's opinion."

**If they push:** "What would the internal version look like? Who'd run it?" — often they realize they don't have that person.

---

### "We don't have customers yet, so we can't use this"

**This is actually a buying trigger, not a blocker:**
> "That's actually the best time to do this. You get to design the journey before customers experience a version you didn't plan.

> Companies that map the journey at first-customer stage have fewer fires to fight at 50 customers. The patterns that cause churn at scale are usually set in the first 10 customers. We help you catch that now."

**Reframe the use case:**
> "For pre-launch companies, CX Mate builds a Sales Journey — first touch through closed deal. You're not missing anything. You're just starting with the right half of the lifecycle for your stage."

---

### "We need to see ROI before we invest in anything"

> "That's exactly why we show you impact projections from the first run. You see the revenue at risk, the calculation, and the assumptions — before you spend anything. The Starter run is free. You either see value in the first 10 minutes or you don't, and nothing is wasted."

**If they want proof from other customers:**
> "We're in beta — I won't pretend we have 3-year case studies. What I can show you is the methodology: every projection uses published industry benchmarks (Bain, SaaStr, Gladly) with the formulas visible. You can challenge any number. That's deliberate."

---

### "We're not sure CX is the priority right now"

> "I hear that a lot. And then I ask: where are you losing customers? And almost nobody can answer that question with confidence.

> That's not a CX problem — that's a visibility problem. You can't prioritize fixing something you can't see. This gives you the visibility. What you do with it is up to you."

**If they're pre-revenue / pre-churn:**
> "Even at your stage, the journey you design now becomes the one that scales. The founders who wait until churn is a problem spend three times as long fixing it. Prevention is cheaper than cure."

---

## The Cardinal Rules of Competitive Conversations

1. Never attack a competitor they already bought — you're criticizing their judgment
2. Never compete on features — we lose to Gainsight on features every time
3. Always reframe the category — we're a journey design platform, not a CS tool
4. Lead with fit — "we're designed for companies without a CX expert" is your strongest asset
5. Validate their current tool, then add the angle we cover that it doesn't
