# CX Mate — Pricing & Monetization Strategic Brief

*Strategic Decision Team · 2026-03-05*

---

## The Problem Statement

CX Mate has Stripe wired, a pricing page live, and zero paying customers. Before we get real users through the door, we need a pricing model that:

1. **Doesn't block feedback** — we need people using the product NOW
2. **Creates a clear upgrade moment** — not a vague "pay for more"
3. **Matches what founders actually value** — not what we think they should value
4. **Is simple enough to explain in one sentence**
5. **Can evolve** — we'll learn what works in the first 30 days

The current model (Free + Starter at $79/mo or $149 one-time) was designed theoretically. It hasn't been tested. This brief examines three options and recommends one.

---

## What We Know

### The Product Delivers Value in Three Moments

| Moment | What Happens | User Emotion |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **Moment 1: The Map** | Journey generated — 20+ stages, meaningful moments, risk flags | "Holy shit, this is my company" |
| **Moment 2: The Confrontation** | CX Report names their blind spots with named patterns | "That's... uncomfortably accurate" |
| **Moment 3: The Playbook** | Specific, prioritized recommendations with impact estimates | "I know what to do Monday" |

**The problem:** Moment 1 is so powerful that people might screenshot and leave. The free tier currently gives away all three moments. What's left to sell?

|------|-------|-------------|----------------|
| Gainsight | $30K-$100K+/year | CS platform | 12-24 weeks |
| ChurnZero | $12K-$50K/year | CS platform | 6-8 weeks |
| Totango | $10K-$30K/year | CS platform | 4-8 weeks |
| Planhat | $15K-$40K/year | CS + Revenue | Variable |
| **CX Mate** | **$0-$149** | **CX intelligence** | **1 conversation** |

CX Mate is 100x cheaper and 100x faster. The question isn't "is it worth $79/mo?" — it's "what justifies paying anything when the free run is this good?"

### Who We're Selling To

- **Founders** (Seed–Series A): $0-$50/mo tolerance for "nice to have", $100-$300/mo for "this is saving me from hiring"
- **Revenue Leaders** (Series A-B): $200-$500/mo budget authority without approval
- **Heads of CS** (Growing): $500-$2K/mo with VP/CEO approval

## Three Options

---

### OPTION A: "Freemium + Monthly" (Current Model, Refined)

**Structure:**
| Tier | Price | What's Included |
|------|-------|----------------|
| **Free** | $0 | Full single run: journey + confrontation + playbook + PDF. No account needed. No saving. |
| **Starter** | $79/mo | Save results. Monthly CX Score refresh. Evidence Wall. Slack nudges. Up to 2 journeys. |
| **Pro** | $249/mo | Unlimited journeys. Review mining (G2/Capterra). HubSpot/Intercom integration. Board deck export. Priority support. |

**Upgrade trigger:** "Your results expire when you close this tab. Save them → create account → $79/mo."

**Pros:**
- Standard SaaS model — founders understand it
- Low entry price for experimentation
- Monthly recurring = predictable revenue

**Cons:**
- $79/mo for "saving results" feels like a storage fee, not a value fee
- Hard to justify monthly payment for a product you might use once per quarter
- The free tier cannibalizes Starter — PDF export means they already saved it

**Revenue model (100 users):**
- 10% convert to Starter = 10 × $79 = $790/mo
- 3% convert to Pro = 3 × $249 = $747/mo
- **Total: ~$1,537/mo MRR**

### OPTION B: "One-Time Purchase + Expansion" (Pay Per Intelligence)

**Structure:**
| Tier | Price | What's Included |
|------|-------|----------------|
| **Preview** | $0 | Partial run: journey map + confrontation (high-level). Playbook locked. No PDF. |
| **Full Analysis** | $149 one-time | Complete run: full confrontation detail + full playbook + PDF + NotebookLM. Permanent access to this analysis. |
| **CX Pro** | $49/month | Monthly CX Score refresh. New journeys. Review mining. Integrations. Ongoing intelligence. |

**Upgrade trigger:** "Your CX Report found 7 patterns. See the full analysis + your action playbook → $149."

**Pros:**
- One-time purchase matches the "I need this now" psychology of founders
- $149 feels like "I'm buying a consultant report" — familiar B2B framing
- Low-friction first purchase → monthly upsell for ongoing value
- No subscription fatigue — they pay for something concrete

**Cons:**
- One-time revenue doesn't compound — need volume
- Have to clearly define what "preview" shows vs. what's locked
- Risk of "I got the report, I'm done" — no retention loop

**Revenue model (100 users):**
- 20% buy Full Analysis = 20 × $149 = $2,980 (one-time)
- 8% of buyers convert to Pro = ~2 × $49 = $98/mo ongoing
- **Month 1: ~$3,078. Month 2+: ~$98/mo + new one-time purchases**

### OPTION C: "Open Beta → Founder Pricing" (Growth-First, Monetize Later)

**Structure:**
| Phase | Price | What's Included | Duration |
|-------|-------|----------------|----------|
| **Beta** | $0 (everything) | Full product, full analysis, saving, PDF — no restrictions | First 100 users or 60 days |
| **Founder pricing** | $29/mo or $199/year | Everything. Locked in forever. "You helped build this." | Post-beta early adopters |
| **GA pricing** | $99/mo or $149 one-time | Standard tiers kick in with clear free/paid split | Post-validation |

**Upgrade trigger:** "Beta ends March 31. Lock in founder pricing ($29/mo forever) or lose access to saved results."

**Pros:**
- Maximum feedback velocity — zero friction to full product
- "Founder pricing" creates urgency and loyalty
- Learn what people actually value before deciding what to charge for
- 100 beta users with full access = real usage data for pricing decisions
- Creates early advocates who feel ownership

**Cons:**
- Zero revenue during beta — can Anat sustain this?
- Risk that "free" anchors the price and $99/mo feels too high later
- "Founder pricing" could become a permanent discount that's hard to walk back
- No pricing validation until beta ends

**Revenue model (100 beta users, converting post-beta):**
- Month 1-2: $0
- 30% lock in founder pricing = 30 × $29 = $870/mo
- **Month 3+: ~$870/mo MRR, growing as new users join at GA pricing**

## Devil's Advocate — Stress Testing Each Option

### Against Option A (Freemium + Monthly):
**"The free tier is too good."** If someone gets the full journey + confrontation + playbook + PDF for free, what exactly is $79/mo buying? "Saving results" is not a value proposition — it's a feature. The upgrade trigger is weak. People will run it, PDF it, and never come back. You'll have 1,000 free users and 15 paying ones.

**The real risk:** You're competing with your own free tier. Every feature you put in Free makes Starter harder to sell.

### Against Option B (One-Time Purchase):
**"You're capping your upside."** $149 once is great for the user, terrible for building a business. SaaS investors value MRR, not one-time sales. And if the "preview" is too thin, no one converts. If it's too generous, no one needs the full version. Finding the right lock point is hard — get it wrong and conversion craters.

**The real risk:** One-time purchases create a "treadmill" — you need new customers every month just to maintain revenue, not grow it.

### Against Option C (Open Beta):
**"You're training people to expect free."** Behavioral economics: once something is free, any price feels like a loss. The jump from $0 to $29/mo is psychologically bigger than $0 to $79/mo because users feel they're losing something they had. Also, "founder pricing" can become a crutch — Slack, Notion, and Figma all struggled to move early users off discounted plans.

**The real risk:** You optimize for user count, not revenue. A startup that can't monetize its first 100 users won't monetize the next 1,000.

## Chief of Staff Synthesis — The Recommendation

### Go with **Option B (One-Time Purchase + Expansion)**, modified.

**Why:**

1. **It matches the product's natural rhythm.** CX Mate is not a daily-use tool. It's a "run it, get the analysis, act on it, come back in 3 months" tool. Monthly subscriptions punish this pattern. One-time purchase rewards it.

2. **$149 one-time is the right anchor.** It positions CX Mate as "the $149 CX consultant" — not a subscription to manage, not a free toy. B2B founders spend $149 without blinking. It's lunch-with-a-mentor money. It's below the "need approval" threshold at most companies.

3. **The preview-to-paid wall is the product's natural climax.** The confrontation page (CX Report) is the emotional peak — that's where you lock the paywall. Show them the pattern names, the risk flags, the "you're losing customers here" diagnosis. Lock the *details* and the *playbook*. They'll pay because they just felt the pain.

4. **Monthly expansion is the real business.** The $49/mo Pro tier is where retention happens: monthly CX Score refresh, review mining, integrations. But this only matters AFTER they've seen the value. The one-time purchase proves value → monthly proves ongoing value.

### The Modified Option B — "The CX Mate Model"

| What They See | Price | What's Included |
|---------------|-------|----------------|
| **Try CX Mate** | $0 | Full onboarding → Journey Map → CX Report (headline patterns + severity, details blurred). No playbook. No PDF. |
| **Get Your Full Analysis** | $149 one-time | Full CX Report with details + Full Playbook + PDF export + NotebookLM export. Results saved permanently. |
| **CX Mate Pro** | $49/mo | Monthly CX Score tracking. Unlimited re-runs. Review mining (G2/Capterra). HubSpot/Intercom data integration. Evidence Wall. Slack nudges. Priority support. Board deck export. |

### The Paywall Moment — Exactly Where It Goes

```
ONBOARDING (free) → JOURNEY MAP (free) → CX REPORT (partial — free)
                                                 ↓
                                    "We found 7 patterns in your CX."
                                    "3 are critical. Here's what's at risk."
                                    [Pattern names visible, details blurred]
                                                 ↓
                                    🔒 "Unlock your full analysis → $149"
                                                 ↓
                              FULL CX REPORT + PLAYBOOK + PDF (paid)
```

**Why this works:**
- The journey map alone is impressive enough to share ("look what this tool generated")
- The CX Report headline patterns create urgency ("I need to know more")
- The playbook is the prize — it's what makes the analysis actionable
- $149 feels like buying a deliverable, not subscribing to software

### Beta Strategy (First 30 Days)

Don't charge yet. Here's the bridge:

1. **Week 1-2:** Everything free. Get 20-30 people through the full flow. Collect feedback.
2. **Week 3-4:** Introduce the paywall on NEW users only. Early users keep full access as "founding members."
3. **Week 5+:** Full pricing live. Founding members get Pro free for 3 months as thank-you.

This gives you feedback velocity WITHOUT training the market to expect free forever. The paywall is introduced as "the product matured" — not "we took something away."
