---

## The 3 Pillars

Most CX programs only measure Experience (Pillar 1). The highest-performing CX leaders — the ones who influence the P&L — also measure Capability and Adoption.

### 💫 Pillar 1: Experience (The "Outcome")
- **What it is:** How the customer perceives the value
- **Key metric:** Value Realization
- **The shift:** Stop asking "How satisfied were you?" → Start asking "How well did this meet the purpose of your visit?"

### ⚙️ Pillar 2: Capability (The "Engine")
- **What it is:** Does your team have the data, tools, and authority to fix the problem?
- **Key metric:** Mean Time to Resolution (MTTR)
- **The reality:** If your CX team identifies friction but it takes 6 months to get a Jira ticket prioritized, your Capability is zero. You're a historian, not a leader.

### 🤖 Pillar 3: Adoption (The "Behavior")
- **What it is:** Are customers (and employees) actually using the better path you built?
- **Key metric:** Digital Self-Service Diversion Rate
- **The reality:** You can build the world's most intuitive AI-chat interface (Experience), but if 80% still call the help desk (Adoption), your CX strategy has failed the business.

---

## Why This Matters

**Old way (reporter):** "Our NPS is up 2 points!"
**New way (operating owner):** "Experience scores are high, but Adoption of the new self-service portal is only at 12%. We're paying for premium experience twice — once for the tech and once for the manual labor. We need to pivot UX focus to onboarding."

One is a narrative. The other is a BUSINESS DECISION.

---

## Leader's Health Check

Run any CX report through this filter:
1. 💫 Experience: Do we know if the customer got what they wanted?
2. ⚙️ Capability: Can we actually fix the root cause when it breaks?
3. 🤖 Adoption: Are they using the low-effort path we designed?

If you only have 1 → you are a **Reporter**
If you have all 3 → you are an **Operating Owner**
