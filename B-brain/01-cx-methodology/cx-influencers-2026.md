## The CX Pioneers (Foundational Strategy)

### Jeanne Bliss
- **Title:** Often called the "Godmother of CX." Pioneered the Chief Customer Officer (CCO) role.
- **Focus:** Leadership, operationalizing empathy, C-suite accountability for CX.
- **Key Work:** *Chief Customer Officer 2.0*
- **Core Framework:** "Five competencies" of customer-centric leadership — honour and manage customers as assets, align around experience, build a customer listening path, proactive experience reliability, and leadership, accountability and culture.
- **Use when:** Executive stakeholders, board-level CX reporting, CCO-level journey ownership, org-wide CX culture.
- **Key Quote:** "Earn the right to grow by improving customers' lives."

### Shep Hyken
- **Title:** Global authority on customer service and loyalty.
- **Focus:** Creating "Moments of Magic" — every touchpoint is an opportunity to exceed expectations. Service culture transformation.
- **Key Work:** *The Amazement Revolution*
- **Core Framework:** "Moments of Magic" vs "Moments of Misery" — categorise every customer interaction. The goal is consistent Amazement, not occasional wow.
- **Use when:** Identifying delight moments in journey mapping, recommending service culture changes, onboarding and support touchpoints.

### Ron Kaufman
- **Title:** Renowned for "Uplifting Service" — large-scale service culture transformation.
- **Focus:** Entire organisations (and even countries) adopting a service mindset.
- **Key Work:** *Uplifting Service*
- **Use when:** Service culture at scale, cross-functional CX alignment, government/enterprise CX.

## The Futurists (AI & Technology)

### Blake Morgan
- **Title:** CX Futurist. Go-to expert for "future-proofing" organisations.
- **Focus:** How technology and AI are reshaping the next generation of customer experiences. Keeping experiences human in an automated world.
- **Use when:** AI tool recommendations, automation decisions, digital CX strategy, self-serve vs high-touch balance.
- **Key Insight:** "The companies that win in the next decade will be those that use AI to handle transactions while freeing humans to handle relationships."

### Steven Van Belleghem
- **Title:** Leading voice on technology + human touch intersection.
- **Focus:** How brands can remain "human" in a world dominated by AI and automation. The "Partners in Life" brand model.
- **Use when:** Recommending AI adoption in customer-facing roles, self-service design, chatbot recommendations.
- **Key Insight:** "AI handles the convenience; humans handle the complexity."

### Jay Baer
- **Title:** Researcher and strategist. "Experience as marketing" evangelist.
- **Focus:** How AI-enabled customer journeys can still feel deeply personal. Customer experience as the new marketing.
- **Key Work:** *Hug Your Haters*
- **Use when:** Customer advocacy stages, turning complaints into loyalty, response-time recommendations.

## The Practitioners (Execution & Journey Mapping)

### Jeannie Walters
- **Title:** CCXP, CSP. Founder & CEO of Experience Investigators. Award-winning CX expert, international keynote speaker, LinkedIn Learning instructor (500K+ professionals trained).
- **Focus:** Shifting organisations from reactive customer service to proactive, company-wide customer experience strategy. CX as a business discipline, not a department. Practical frameworks for cross-functional teams.
- **Key Work:** *Experience Is Everything: Making Every Moment Count in the Age of Customer Expectations* (April 2026, Simon & Schuster). LinkedIn Learning courses on CX strategy.
- **Core Framework:** CX Mission Statement, CX Success Blueprint, and the "Four C's of CX Culture." Designs CX programmes that turn customer insight into prioritised initiatives with clear owners and measurable outcomes.
- **Use when:** CX strategy as business discipline, proactive vs reactive CX, cross-functional CX ownership, CX culture building, AI + human balance in CX.
- **Key Insight:** "Customer experience is no longer a department — it's a business discipline that every role needs to understand." / "AI should amplify human capability, not replace it."
- **Clients:** SAP, Verizon, SurveyMonkey, Citrix — but her frameworks apply at any scale.
- **Recognition:** CX Network Top 40 Future of CX Leaders 2026. LinkedIn most influential CX voice.

### Ian Golding
- **Title:** One of the most respected CX practitioners globally. Key figure in the CCXP (Certified Customer Experience Professional) community.
- **Focus:** Measurable, sustainable CX strategy. Rigorous methodology grounded in global standards.
- **Use when:** Defining CX metrics, measurement frameworks, CCXP methodology references, maturity assessments.
- **Key Insight:** "CX strategy without measurement is just good intentions."

### Annette Franz
- **Title:** Expert in journey mapping and customer-centric culture.
- **Focus:** Understanding the "why" behind customer behaviour. Turning data into action. Voice of the Customer programmes.
- **Key Work:** *Customer Understanding: Three Ways to Put the "Customer" in Customer Experience (and at the Heart of Your Business)*
- **Use when:** Journey mapping methodology, pain point identification, VoC programmes, NPS follow-up processes.
- **Key Insight:** "Journey mapping is not about the map. It's about the insights you gain from building it."

### Tiffani Bova
- **Title:** Former Salesforce growth lead. Explorer of the EX↔CX link.
- **Focus:** The critical connection between Employee Experience (EX) and Customer Experience (CX). Internal culture drives external experience.
- **Key Work:** *The Experience Mindset*
- **Use when:** Internal process recommendations, CS team enablement, culture recommendations, manager-level plays.
- **Key Insight:** "You can't deliver a great customer experience without a great employee experience."

## How to Use in CX Mate Prompts

The AI prompt should reference these frameworks with light authority — not heavy academic citation. Examples:

- *"Following Annette Franz's journey mapping approach, the first step is understanding WHY customers behave this way, not just WHAT they do."*
- *"Ian Golding (CCXP) identifies this as a critical moment of truth — the point where customers form a lasting opinion."*
- *"This is what Shep Hyken calls a 'Moment of Magic' — a touchpoint where you can consistently exceed expectations without heroic effort."*
- *"Blake Morgan would flag this as a risk: automation that removes the human from a high-emotion moment."*
