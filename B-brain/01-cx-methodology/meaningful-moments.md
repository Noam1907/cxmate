# Meaningful Moments Taxonomy

## Moment Types

### Moments of Truth
Interactions where the customer forms a lasting opinion about your company.

**Examples (B2B SaaS):**
- First impression of the product (demo or trial)
- First support ticket experience
- Renewal conversation
- First escalation handling
- Champion introduction to their boss

### Moments of Pain
Known friction points that cause frustration or abandonment.

**Examples (B2B SaaS):**
- Complex onboarding with no guidance
- Unclear pricing or surprise charges
- Slow support response (>4 hours for critical)
- Feature gaps discovered post-purchase
- Data migration difficulties

### Moments of Delight
Opportunities to exceed expectations and create loyalty.

**Examples (B2B SaaS):**
- Proactive check-in before customer asks for help
- Celebrating customer milestones (100th user, 1-year anniversary)
- Personalized tips based on usage patterns
- Fast resolution of critical issues
- Sharing relevant industry insights

### Moments of Risk
Signals that predict churn or escalation if unaddressed.

**Examples (B2B SaaS):**
- Silence (no login for 14+ days)
- Declining usage trend (week over week)
- Negative sentiment in communications
- Champion leaves the company
- Support ticket volume spike
- Competitor evaluation signals

## Severity Scoring

| Severity | Color | Definition | Action Required |
|----------|-------|------------|----------------|
| Critical | Red | Directly causes churn if mishandled | Immediate (same day) |
| High | Orange | Significantly impacts satisfaction | Within 48 hours |
| Medium | Yellow | Affects perception but recoverable | Within 1 week |
| Low | Green | Nice-to-optimize | Next sprint/quarter |

## Recommendation Framework

For each meaningful moment, define:

1. **What:** The specific action to take
2. **When:** Exact timing trigger (day X, event Y, signal Z)
3. **How:** Communication template or process step
4. **Why:** The CX principle behind this recommendation
5. **Measure:** How to know if this is working
