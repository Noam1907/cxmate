# CX Mate — ICP Profile

Who we build for. Who we message to. Who we say no to.

---

## The Core ICP (In One Sentence)

**B2B companies that haven't hired a CX expert yet** — stage-based, not size-based. A 600-person company with no CX function is a perfect customer. A 30-person company with a dedicated CX hire is not.

---

## ICP Segmentation by Maturity Stage

CX Mate's onboarding is maturity-adaptive. The ICP isn't one company — it's a stage of organizational development.

| Stage | Label | Who They Are | What They Have | What They Need |
|-------|-------|--------------|----------------|----------------|
| **Pre-launch** | Building | Founder, small team, product not live yet | Vision, no data | A CX framework before bad habits form |
| **First customers** | Proving | 1-50 customers, founder-led CX | Anecdotes, Slack, Google Sheets | Know which moments matter before they scale |
| **Growing** | Scaling | 51-500 customers, first CS hire or none | Some CRM data, gut feel, reactive support | Standardized playbook, reduce churn, unify sales and CS |
| **Scaling** | Optimizing | 500+ customers, growing team, fragmented tools | NPS, multiple tools, siloed data | Intelligence layer to optimize across the full lifecycle |

## Primary Personas

### 1. The Founder (Pre-launch → First customers)

**Demographics:**
- Title: CEO, Co-founder, Founder
- Company: Seed to Series A, 2-30 people
- Vertical: Any B2B — SaaS initial focus, expanding to services, agencies, FinTech

**Their World:**
- Building the product AND selling it AND supporting it — simultaneously
- Customer experience is handled through founder memory and Slack DMs
- Hasn't thought of it as "CX" — thinks of it as "making customers successful"
- Losing early customers without knowing why

**Their Pain:**
- "We closed 12 customers and 3 churned. I don't know if it's the product, the onboarding, or how we're setting expectations."
- "I know every customer by name. That won't work at 50 customers. I need a playbook."
- "I can't afford a CX consultant or a Gainsight implementation. I need a shortcut."

**Their Goal:**
- CX foundation in place in minutes, not months
- Know which moments matter before they scale
- Stop reinventing the wheel for every new customer

**How they find us:** LinkedIn, founder communities, word of mouth, investor recommendations

**What makes them sign up:** They see their own company reflected back at them — specific maturity stage, specific pain, specific next action. Not generic advice.

---

### 2. The Revenue Leader / CRO (Growing → Scaling)

**Demographics:**
- Title: CRO, VP Sales, VP Revenue, COO
- Company: Series A-B, 50-500 people, $1M-$20M ARR
- Owns: full funnel from first touch to renewal

**Their World:**
- Sales and CS are separate teams, handoffs are broken
- Revenue is leaking at the deal-to-onboarding handoff and at renewals
- No unified view from first contact to advocacy
- Reporting on lagging indicators (churn rate) not leading indicators (moment health)

**Their Pain:**
- "We're closing deals but losing customers in the first 90 days. Something breaks between sales and CS."
- "I can't tell which moments in the customer journey are high-risk until it's too late."
- "I need a number I can take to the board — what's the revenue impact of improving our CX?"

**Their Goal:**
- One unified map from first touch to advocacy
- Quantified revenue impact of CX improvements (they speak $ not NPS)
- Reduce revenue leakage at handoff points

**How they find us:** LinkedIn, RevOps communities, CS tools comparison searches, peer recommendations

**What makes them sign up:** The transparent impact projections — real numbers, real formulas, their data vs benchmarks.

---

### 3. The Head of CS / VP Customer Success (Growing → Scaling)

**Demographics:**
- Title: Head of CS, VP Customer Success, Director of Customer Experience
- Company: Series A-C, 30-300 customers, team of 3-20 CSMs
- Owns: onboarding, retention, expansion, health scoring

**Their World:**
- Managing customer health reactively — firefighting, not preventing fires
- No standardized playbook — each CSM does it differently
- Team growing faster than processes
- NPS score exists but doesn't tell them what to do

**Their Pain:**
- "We have NPS but I don't know which moments drive it up or down."
- "New CSMs don't know what good looks like. I have no playbook to give them."
- "We're reactively managing churn. I need to see risks before customers tell me they're leaving."

**Their Goal:**
- Proactive engagement based on moment health, not lagging indicators
- Standardized playbook every CSM can follow
- Evidence-backed recommendations tied to their actual customer profile

**How they find us:** CS communities (ChurnZero ecosystem, Gainsight PulseConference, LinkedIn CS groups), Google searches for "customer success playbook template"

**What makes them sign up:** The evidence annotations — recommendations that link back to their specific pain points, competitors, and context. Not generic advice.

## Who We Are NOT Building For

| Profile | Why Not |
|---------|---------|
| Companies with a dedicated CX team (CX Manager, VP CX) | They have the expertise. They need tools, not a foundation. |
| Enterprise (1000+ employees, established CX function) | Gainsight, Qualtrics, Medallia serve them. We're the path before enterprise. |
| B2C companies | Journey dynamics, moments, and playbooks are fundamentally different. |
| Agencies building CX for clients | Different buying behavior, different value proposition. |
| Companies seeking compliance-driven CX (healthcare, finance with regulatory CX) | Requires domain-specific expertise beyond our scope. |

## Buying Triggers

What makes an ICP customer buy *right now* vs "maybe later":

| Trigger | What it Signals |
|---------|----------------|
| First churn event | They lost a customer and don't know why |
| Hiring first CS person | Need a playbook to hand them |
| Series A closing | Investors expect repeatability and process |
| Onboarding complaints | Customer feedback is arriving and it's not good |
| Sales-CS conflict | Handoff tension is becoming visible to leadership |
| Competitor win | A customer churned to a competitor with better CX |
| Board asks about NPS | Suddenly need to explain customer experience in numbers |

## ICP Anti-Patterns to Avoid in Messaging

- Don't lead with "AI" — founders don't buy AI, they buy outcomes
- Don't lead with "platform" — sounds expensive and complex
- Don't say "5-300 employees" — stage-based, not size-based
- Don't promise "enterprise CX" — they don't want enterprise, they want accessible
- Don't use NPS jargon — most early-stage founders don't have NPS yet
- Don't say "data-driven" — SMBs hear "cold and corporate." Say "train your instincts" instead
- Don't claim a specific minute count — "in minutes, not months" is the right comparison frame
