## What CX Mate Sounds Like

### The 4 Principles

**1. Talk to the founder, not the market**
Every line is written to one person sitting at their laptop at 10pm, worried about churn. Not to "B2B SaaS companies." To *them*.

| Don't | Do |
|-------|-----|
| "CX Mate helps B2B startups optimize their customer experience lifecycle." | "You're losing customers. You just don't know exactly where yet." |
| "Our platform provides actionable insights." | "Here's what's breaking — and what to do about it Monday." |

**2. Name the pain before the solution**
Founders don't trust solutions that skip the problem. Start with what's broken. Let them feel seen. Then offer the fix.

| Don't | Do |
|-------|-----|
| "Get a complete customer journey map in 5 minutes." | "Your customers have a journey whether you designed it or not. Find out what it actually looks like." |

**3. Be specific or be silent**
Generic claims destroy trust. Every claim needs a number, a name, or a mechanism.

| Don't | Do |
|-------|-----|
| "Improve your customer experience." | "Map 50+ meaningful moments across your entire customer lifecycle." |
| "Significant revenue impact." | "$180K–$340K in recoverable annual revenue." |

**4. Earn the next click**
Every screen's job is to earn the next action — not to explain everything, not to impress. To earn the next step.

## The CX Mate Persona

CX Mate presents as a **CCXP-certified peer advisor** — not a consultant, not a chatbot, not a SaaS platform. A peer who's been through this before and wants to help you not make the same mistakes.

**It feels like:**
- The CX expert your investor told you to hire, available at 2am
- A peer who built CX at a company like yours and came back to help
- Someone who did the research before the meeting so the meeting is actually useful

**It does NOT feel like:**
- An enterprise software vendor
- A generic AI tool ("I can help you with many tasks!")
- A consultant padding hours
- A CX textbook

## Vocabulary

### Use these words
- **Journey** (not "path" or "flow" or "funnel")
- **Moments** (not "touchpoints" — too corporate)
- **Playbook** (not "roadmap" or "action plan")
- **Map / Mapping** (active, visual, exploration)
- **Founder** (for early-stage; "team" when growing)
- **Real** (as in: "real analysis", "real data", "real impact")
- **Confrontation** (the page name — intentional, brave, not scary)
- **At your stage** (peer framing, not generic advice)
- **Companies like yours** (specific, not universal)

### Avoid these words
- Platform / Solution / Suite
- Optimize / Streamline / Leverage
- Enterprise-grade / Best-in-class / World-class
- Unlock (overused across SaaS)
- Seamless (especially for onboarding — nothing is seamless)
- "Tool" — CX Mate is an intelligence system, not a tool
- "We" in product copy — use "you" language
- Free (replaced with value signals: "results in 5 minutes", "no setup required")

### Avoid these patterns
- Passive voice: "insights are generated" → "we map your journey"
- Vague time: "quickly" → "in minutes" (never a specific minute count — the comparison is weeks/months, not other minutes)
- Hedging: "may help" → "will show you"
- Feature-first: "AI-powered journey mapping" → "know where customers are leaving and why"

## CTA Copy Rules

CTAs are decisions, not buttons. Match the emotional state and stakes.

| Context | Weak CTA | Strong CTA |
|---------|----------|------------|
| Landing page hero | "Get Started" | "Get Your Action Playbook" |
| After CX Report | "Continue" | "See Where You're Losing Them" |
| Playbook empty state | "Generate" | "Build My Action Plan" |
| End of journey map | "Next" | "Get the Playbook" |
| Dashboard, no data | "Begin" | "Generate Your First Journey" |

**Rule:** The CTA should complete this sentence: "I want to ___."

## The Story Arc

CX Mate is a product with chapters. Every screen has a narrative job:

| Screen | Narrative Job | Reader's Emotional State |
|--------|--------------|--------------------------|
| Landing page | "This product understands my problem" | Skeptical → Curious |
| Onboarding | "This is worth my 5 minutes" | Curious → Invested |
| Generation loading | "Something real is being built for me" | Invested → Anticipating |
| CX Report | "They figured out what's actually wrong" | Anticipating → Confronted |
| Journey Map | "This is my company, mapped" | Confronted → Seeing clearly |
| Playbook | "I know exactly what to do on Monday" | Seeing clearly → Empowered |
| Dashboard | "I'm making progress. This is working." | Empowered → Committed |

Never write copy that belongs to the wrong chapter. The Playbook isn't the place to explain what CX Mate is.

## The Monetization Reframe

"Free" has been removed from the product. Copy must do different work — value signals replace free signals.

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| "Free. No account needed." | "Results in minutes, not months. No setup required." |
| "Try for free" | "See your journey" / "Start mapping" |
| "Free tier" | "Starter" / "Explore" |

The implicit message: *this is worth your time and, eventually, your money.* Confident, not apologetic.

## How to Write About the AI Layer

CX Mate is AI-powered — but *how* we describe that matters enormously. Wrong framing makes us sound like every other "AI-powered" SaaS. Right framing makes us sound like a CX expert who happened to bring their intelligence system.

### What to say (the behind-the-scenes that earns trust)

| Weak framing | Strong framing |
|-------------|---------------|
| "AI-generated recommendations" | "Reasoned from your specific journey and risks — not a template" |
| "Personalized to your company" | "Built around the tools you already use, plus what you're missing" |
| "Export your playbook" | "Open in NotebookLM — turn this into a board deck, onboarding doc, or QBR in minutes" |
| "Powered by Claude" | Mention CCXP methodology and measurement baked in — the model is implementation detail |
| "Templates included" | Never say "templates" — it reads as Word docs. Say "AI-generated assets" or "ready to extend with any AI tool" |

### The 4 AI signals that create the aha moment

When users ask "how is this different from Gemini?", they need to *feel* the answer in the product — not just read it. These 4 signals do that work:

1. **"Wiring in: [their tool]"** — Showing the user's HubSpot/Intercom on the loading screen proves we read what they told us.
2. **Measurement per stage** — NPS/CSAT/CES checkpoints aren't something you'd think to ask for. When they see it, they feel the methodology depth.
3. **Evidence annotations** — "From your input: customers leaving without warning" tied to specific recommendations proves the journey is *theirs*, not generic.
4. **NotebookLM/AI export** — This reframes CX Mate as an intelligence layer that feeds other AI tools, not a standalone SaaS.

### Never write copy that sounds like a prompt

Bad: "AI analyzes your business and generates a personalized customer journey map."
Good: "We looked at [Company] before you answered a single question. Here's what we found — and here's where your journey has gaps."

The AI should feel invisible until it surfaces something the founder didn't expect. Then it should feel like magic.

## Anti-Patterns to Flag

When reviewing copy, flag anything that:
- Could be said by any SaaS product without changing a word
- Explains the feature instead of the transformation
- Uses an adjective where a number would work
- Addresses "companies" instead of "you"
- Puts the solution before the pain
- Claims a specific minute count for time-to-value (the comparison is weeks/months, not minutes)
- Names the process instead of the outcome in CTAs ("Map Your Journey" → "Get Your Action Playbook")

## CX Professional Vocabulary

The language CX Mate uses signals whether we're credible or corporate. This vocabulary guide ensures every agent, prompt, and UI string sounds like a real CX professional — not a SaaS marketing deck.

### Terms We Use (organized by domain)

**Core CX Concepts**
- Customer Journey — the full end-to-end experience, not just a funnel
- Touchpoints — every interaction point between customer and company
- Voice of the Customer (VoC) — structured listening, not just surveys
- Customer-Centric — designing around the customer's world, not ours
- Customer Effort Score (CES) — how hard was it?
- Net Promoter Score (NPS) — would they recommend?

**Customer Insight & Feedback**
- Closed-Loop Feedback — hear it, act on it, tell them what changed
- Sentiment Analysis — reading the emotion behind the data
- Customer Segmentation — treating different customers differently (on purpose)
- Behavioral Signals — what they do matters more than what they say
- Proactive Outreach — reaching out before they reach out to you

**Measurement & Metrics**
- First Contact Resolution (FCR) — solved in one interaction
- Time to Value (TTV) — how fast they get the aha moment
- Customer Lifetime Value (CLV/LTV) — the full revenue picture
- Churn Rate — who's leaving and how fast
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) — the growth-within-growth metric

**Retention & Growth**
- Customer Advocacy — when customers sell for you
- Expansion Revenue — growing within existing accounts
- Renewal Rate — the ultimate trust metric
- Customer Health Score — composite signal for risk/opportunity
- Cohort Analysis — comparing groups over time

**CX Operations**
- Journey Orchestration — coordinating actions across touchpoints
- Service Recovery — turning failures into loyalty
- Escalation Path — clear routing when things go wrong
- Handoff — the moment between teams where customers feel the cracks
- Playbook — specific, repeatable actions (not a "strategy deck")

**CX Strategy**
- Experience Design — intentional, not accidental
- Customer Intelligence — data that drives decisions, not dashboards
- Outcome-Based Thinking — measuring what changed, not what was delivered
- Revenue Impact — CX in the language the board speaks

### Overused Buzzwords to Avoid

These terms signal to experienced CX leaders that you're parroting marketing copy, not speaking from practice. **Never use these in CX Mate output, UI, or prompts.**

**Vague amplifiers (say nothing)**
- Synergy / Synergize
- Leverage (as a verb — say "use" or "build on")
- Best-in-Class / World-Class
- Cutting-edge / Next-generation
- Paradigm Shift

**Empty promises**
- Seamless (nothing is seamless — say "smooth" or "low-friction" if you must)
- Frictionless (same — be honest about effort)
- 360-Degree View (nobody has this; say "complete picture" or "full context")
- Omnichannel (say "across channels" or name the specific channels)
- Holistic (say "complete" or "end-to-end")

**Corporate jargon that distances**
- Ecosystem (unless you mean an actual platform ecosystem)
- Scalable (say what scales and how)
- Disruptive / Game-Changer
- Empower (show the power, don't claim it)
- Unlock (the most overused SaaS verb — say "reveal", "surface", or "find")

**Meaningless modifiers**
- Robust / Enterprise-Grade
- Innovative / Transformative
- Actionable Insights (everything should be actionable — just say "insights" or better, say what the insight IS)
- Deep Dive (say "detailed analysis" or just go deep)
- Low-Hanging Fruit (say "quick wins" or "easy first steps")
