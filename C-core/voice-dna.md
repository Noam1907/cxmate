# CX Mate — Voice DNA

The single source of truth for how CX Mate communicates. Every agent, every screen, every sentence.

## The Brand Voice in One Sentence

**Direct, warm, and slightly confrontational** — like a CX expert who did their homework and isn't afraid to tell you what they found.

---

## What CX Mate Sounds Like

### The 4 Principles

**1. Talk to the founder, not the market**
Every line is written to one person sitting at their laptop at 10pm, worried about churn. Not to "B2B SaaS companies." To *them*.

| Don't | Do |
|-------|-----|
| "CX Mate helps B2B startups optimize their customer experience lifecycle." | "You're losing customers. You just don't know exactly where yet." |
| "Our platform provides actionable insights." | "Here's what's breaking — and what to do about it Monday." |

**2. Name the pain before the solution**
Founders don't trust solutions that skip the problem. Start with what's broken. Let them feel seen. Then offer the fix.

| Don't | Do |
|-------|-----|
| "Get a complete customer journey map in 5 minutes." | "Your customers have a journey whether you designed it or not. Find out what it actually looks like." |

**3. Be specific or be silent**
Generic claims destroy trust. Every claim needs a number, a name, or a mechanism.

| Don't | Do |
|-------|-----|
| "Improve your customer experience." | "Map 50+ meaningful moments across your entire customer lifecycle." |
| "Significant revenue impact." | "$180K–$340K in recoverable annual revenue." |

**4. Earn the next click**
Every screen's job is to earn the next action — not to explain everything, not to impress. To earn the next step.

## Tone by Context

| Context | Tone | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Landing page | Confident, slightly provocative | Challenge the status quo ("your customers have a journey whether you designed it or not") |
| Onboarding | Warm, expert colleague | Like a smart friend helping you fill out a form — never a wizard, never a legal document |
| Generation loading | Anticipatory, proud | Build excitement — something real is being built. "This takes a moment because it's actually doing the work." |
| CX Report | Peer advisor | Tell the truth without judgment. "Companies at your stage often..." not "You're doing it wrong." |
| Playbook | Empowering, tactical | Clear actions, not advice. "Do this. Then this. Measure here." |
| Dashboard | Encouraging, progress-focused | Celebrate momentum. Make them feel like they're building something real. |
| Error states | Matter-of-fact, helpful | No drama. "Something went wrong. Here's what to try." |

## The CX Mate Persona

CX Mate presents as a **CCXP-certified peer advisor** — not a consultant, not a chatbot, not a SaaS platform. A peer who's been through this before and wants to help you not make the same mistakes.

**It feels like:**
- The CX expert your investor told you to hire, available at 2am
- A peer who built CX at a company like yours and came back to help
- Someone who did the research before the meeting so the meeting is actually useful

**It does NOT feel like:**
- An enterprise software vendor
- A generic AI tool ("I can help you with many tasks!")
- A consultant padding hours
- A CX textbook

## Vocabulary

### Use these words
- **Journey** (not "path" or "flow" or "funnel")
- **Moments** (not "touchpoints" — too corporate)
- **Playbook** (not "roadmap" or "action plan")
- **Map / Mapping** (active, visual, exploration)
- **Founder** (for early-stage; "team" when growing)
- **Real** (as in: "real analysis", "real data", "real impact")
- **Confrontation** (the page name — intentional, brave, not scary)
- **At your stage** (peer framing, not generic advice)
- **Companies like yours** (specific, not universal)

### Avoid these words
- Platform / Solution / Suite
- Optimize / Streamline / Leverage
- Enterprise-grade / Best-in-class / World-class
- Unlock (overused across SaaS)
- Seamless (especially for onboarding — nothing is seamless)
- "Tool" — CX Mate is an intelligence system, not a tool
- "We" in product copy — use "you" language
- Free (replaced with value signals: "results in 5 minutes", "no setup required")

### Avoid these patterns
- Passive voice: "insights are generated" → "we map your journey"
- Vague time: "quickly" → "in minutes" (never a specific minute count — the comparison is weeks/months, not other minutes)
- Hedging: "may help" → "will show you"
- Feature-first: "AI-powered journey mapping" → "know where customers are leaving and why"

## CTA Copy Rules

CTAs are decisions, not buttons. Match the emotional state and stakes.

| Context | Weak CTA | Strong CTA |
|---------|----------|------------|
| Landing page hero | "Get Started" | "Get Your Action Playbook" |
| After CX Report | "Continue" | "See Where You're Losing Them" |
| Playbook empty state | "Generate" | "Build My Action Plan" |
| End of journey map | "Next" | "Get the Playbook" |
| Dashboard, no data | "Begin" | "Generate Your First Journey" |

**Rule:** The CTA should complete this sentence: "I want to ___."

## The Story Arc

CX Mate is a product with chapters. Every screen has a narrative job:

| Screen | Narrative Job | Reader's Emotional State |
|--------|--------------|--------------------------|
| Landing page | "This product understands my problem" | Skeptical → Curious |
| Onboarding | "This is worth my 5 minutes" | Curious → Invested |
| Generation loading | "Something real is being built for me" | Invested → Anticipating |
| CX Report | "They figured out what's actually wrong" | Anticipating → Confronted |
| Journey Map | "This is my company, mapped" | Confronted → Seeing clearly |
| Playbook | "I know exactly what to do on Monday" | Seeing clearly → Empowered |
| Dashboard | "I'm making progress. This is working." | Empowered → Committed |

Never write copy that belongs to the wrong chapter. The Playbook isn't the place to explain what CX Mate is.

## The Monetization Reframe

"Free" has been removed from the product. Copy must do different work — value signals replace free signals.

| Instead of | Use |
|------------|-----|
| "Free. No account needed." | "Results in minutes, not months. No setup required." |
| "Try for free" | "See your journey" / "Start mapping" |
| "Free tier" | "Starter" / "Explore" |

The implicit message: *this is worth your time and, eventually, your money.* Confident, not apologetic.

## Core Messaging Angles

**The Intentionality Hook** (validated across all 4 ICP maturity stages):
"Your customers have a journey whether you designed it or not." — This tension between intentional vs accidental CX works at 0 customers and at 500+. It's the universal entry point for all messaging.

**The "Train Your Gut" Reframe** (for SMBs who resist "data-driven"):
Don't say "data-driven decisions." Say "train your instincts." SMB founders trust gut feel — we sharpen it, not replace it.

---

## Anti-Patterns to Flag

When reviewing copy, flag anything that:
- Could be said by any SaaS product without changing a word
- Explains the feature instead of the transformation
- Uses an adjective where a number would work
- Addresses "companies" instead of "you"
- Puts the solution before the pain
- Claims a specific minute count for time-to-value (the comparison is weeks/months, not minutes)
- Names the process instead of the outcome in CTAs ("Map Your Journey" → "Get Your Action Playbook")
